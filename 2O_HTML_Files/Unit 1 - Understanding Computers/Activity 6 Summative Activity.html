Building a Dream Computer

Now that we have reviewed all the essential components of a computer system, you will have a chance to (virtually) build one for a particular user. You may choose a user profile from the list provided, or create your own profile (must be approved by your teacher!)

  • a student on a fixed budget
  • a home business owner
  • a gaming enthusiast
  • a photographer
  • a home video enthusiast
  • a distance education student
  • a human resources manager
  • an accountant

Assignment: Build a Dream Computer

Summative Assignment

You must carefully consider what hardware will be required (including any peripherals or AV equipment needed), what operating system will best support this user, what software they will need (you must ensure it can run on your chosen operating system!), what networking equipment and services they will need, and what products or services your user will require for maintenance and security purposes. More details for each of these topics is provided below.

Part A: Hardware

Will you build the computer from parts or purchase a pre-built system? Desktop or laptop? Do you need to buy your user a keyboard, mouse or screen? Will your user need a printer, scanner, speakers or microphone? What about other specialized components? Consider ergonomics in your purchases too! For each component you buy, you must include:

  • A picture of the component that includes the name & price (use a screenshot tool if needed)
  • A link to the store website or online retailer where you could purchase the component.
  • Justification for why this component is needed for your particular user. This justification should include any relevant specifications about the component (speed, size, compatibility with other components, etc)

Record all the costs of the components on the spreadsheet provided on the second evidence card! This spreadsheet will add up all the costs, calculate HST and give a total!

Part B: Operating System

Does your system from Part A come with an operating system already installed? Will you be installing and configuring the OS yourself? Consider the strengths and weaknesses of each operating system (remember your Activity 3 task?) and make sure your operating system will meet your user's needs.

Write a brief paragraph justifying your choice. Be sure to note any additional costs (if applicable) that were not accounted for in Part A on your spreadsheet.

Part C: Software

What application software will your user need? Will they need productivity, graphics, multimedia, network, Internet or development applications? Will you splurge for paid versions or make use of (reputable) free software? For each software application or suite (bundle of applications) you must provide:

  • A image for the software (usually a logo)
  • A link to the store website or online retailer where you could purchase and/or download the software.
  • A screenshot or list of the system requirements for the software. Make sure it will run on the hardware and OS you have selected!
  • Justification of why this software is needed for your particular user.

Record any costs of the software that your user requires on your spreadsheet. If some of the software includes a subscription component, place the cost in the correct column of the spreadsheet (monthly or yearly)

Part D: Networking

Will your user require Internet access? A home network? Wireless connectivity? In this part you will research ISPs (Internet Service Providers) available in your area and choose an Internet package that will meet your user's needs. You will also need to describe what additional networking hardware your user will require (is a modem rental fee included in the contract? Do you need a separate router, hub/switch or wireless access point? Don't forget about cabling, if needed!)

Write a brief paragraph justifying your choices. Include screenshots for any component you buy, as well as a link to the website. Record the total startup cost of networking this computer (hardware & connection fees), plus what your user will have to pay monthly for maintaining the service in your spreadsheet.

Part E: Maintenance & Security

Finally, which software or products will your user need to help maintain this computer? Consider: antivirus or anti-malware software, backups, firewalls, cleaning products, etc. Again, include screenshots for any software or product you buy, as well as a link to the website. Include the costs, including any monthly/yearly costs for subscription services, that your user will pay for maintenance & security on their system on your spreadsheet.

Your assignment must include a spreadsheet with all costs for the new computer system, as well as all relevant recurring costs (monthly, yearly, etc) so your user can give their final approval. While there is no set budget for your 'purchases', you must keep in mind the likely financial situation of the user that you have chosen. (i.e. consider the accountant that must justify her purchases to her supervisor, the home business owner who needs to get a tax write-off from the government, the teenager who has to convince his parents, etc ...) So shop around and look for good deals!

Use the evidence cards provided on Hapara for your submission.
