Your final project for this course is worth 10% of your final grade and will allow you to use and demonstrate the skills you have developed in this course.

Step 1: Think of a program

Your task will be to create a program of your choosing. This program could be a game, a quiz, a utility application that could help someone accomplish a task, or something else that you are interested in creating. You program may use graphics with the pygame library, or be entirely text-based.

Step 2: Plan your program

Those who fail to plan, plan to fail" (attributed to Benjamin Franklin)

Once your proposal has been received, you may begin your design.

Consider how to break down your idea into major tasks and sub-tasks. Plan your work so that it can be completed in stages. If you run out of time and cannot complete all the tasks, you will still have a working program with fewer features. START SMALL, GET IT WORKING, THEN ADD MORE! Clearly identify the tasks and stages that you will need to develop your program.

The design must also include a mock up (simulated screenshots) of what you intend the final version of your program to look like when it runs. Plan your user prompts if doing a text-based game or create some images (MS paint, Google Draw) if you wish to tackle something graphical.

You will communicate the design of your program with flowcharts, pseudocode, or IPO charts as needed. Consider what would be the best tool to fully explain how your program should function to a third party. This will give you another way to think through your program BEFORE coding.

Submit your design document (tasks & stages, mock-up, other design techniques) to the Step 2: Planning evidence card in Hapara.

Step 4: Video Demo

Once you are happy with your program, oyu will create a video demo of your program to share with your teacher and classmates.

Your demo video should meet the following criteria:
  • It should be between 2 - 5 minutes in length
  • You should be explaining what is happening from the user's perspective as you run the program
  • Your program should show a few different scenarios that might arise, and how your program will handle them
  • Your video should be engaging and encourage the viewer to want to give it a try!

Use the flipgrid link on the Step 4: Video Demo evidence card to share your video demo.
